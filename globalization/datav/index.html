This page is about the global migration to the UK. Immigration can promote diversity in the UK.

Migration data is from “THE GLOBAL FLOW OF PEOPLE”. This data divides all countries and regions in the world into 10 regions: North America, Africa, Europe, Fmr Soviet onion, West Asia, South Asia, East Asia, South East Asia, Oceania and Latin America. Each "airplane" flight route represents the trajectory of immigration to the UK.


Introduction of this page:


1. Users can view the immigration trajectory of the region of interest by clicking the label on the left.


2. The bar chart in the lower right corner shows the 16 countries with the largest number of immigrants to the UK between 2005 and 2010. When users move the mouse over the bar graph, they can view the data of these countries.


3. When users click "airplane" with the mouse, they can view the country name and the number of immigrants in this immigration track.


4. When users click the "Replay" button in the upper right corner, the animation of the immigration track can be played again.
